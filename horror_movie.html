Stop motion movies are a style of animation where objects are manipulated in small increments between individually photographed frames, creating the illusion of movement when played back. This technique was popularized in classic films like "The Nightmare Before Christmas" and "Corpse Bride." To create a stop motion movie, animators use puppets or objects and painstakingly move them a small amount between each frame. The images are then played back in sequence, giving the appearance of fluid motion. Many modern filmmakers have used this technique to create unique and captivating animated films.
